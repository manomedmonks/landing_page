FAQ

What is an IVF?

IVF treatment procedure involves the collection of matured eggs and sperms to be combined outside the body in an artificial environment such as of a laboratory. This leads to the formation of an embryo, which is implanted in the woman's uterus.

When an IVF procedure becomes a necessity?

IVF treatment is used to treat a variety of infertility or genetic problems.

In many cases, IVF is considered as a primary treatment for infertility in women over 40 years. Furthermore, IVF can be performed if you have health conditions including,

1. Damage in the fallopian tube or a blockage does not allow the egg to get

fertilized or for an embryo to reach the woman's uterus.

2. Infrequent ovulation will produce fewer eggs available for fertilization.

3. When the uterine tissue implants and grow outside the uterus, termed as Endometriosis, can affect the function of ovaries, fallopian tubes as well as uterus.

4. Loss of normal ovarian function before 40 years can lead to premature ovarian failure. And when the ovaries fail to function normally, they are unable to produce normal amounts of estrogen in the woman's body.

5. Due to benign tumors in the uterus wall, called as fibroids, the procedure of implantation of the fertilized egg can get interfered with.

6. Impaired sperm production, poor mobility or abnormalities in sperm size or shape can interfere with the fertilization process.

Besides, infertility can be caused by certain genetic problems as well.

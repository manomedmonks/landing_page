Medical Treatment In India

Who could have speculated ten years back that India would develop into one of the world's most dominating medical tourism destination? Driven by a flooding economy, an overflow of experienced medical professionals, low treatment costs and a demonstrated national propensity for universal redistributing of client benefit, today India is the leading healthcare facilitator in the world.

Foreign medical tourists can save up to 85% by receiving high-quality treatment in India. Majority of patients travel Indian for undergoing high-acuity procedures like heart and joint surgery. Indian hospitals serve more than 300,000 international medical tourists annually; the vast subcontinent has become a magnet for international medical travelers in its major cities like Bangalore, Delhi, Chennai, Hyderabad, and Mumbai. Medical Treatment in India has advanced because of this popularity in the past decade.

Production of Medical Professionals in India

Unlike the Asian counterparts, that traditionally encouraged medical tourism in their country by aggressively employing top-of-the-line healthcare professions from other nationalities, India produces some of the best and worlds finest doctors and surgeons internally, with the accessibility of excellent in-country teaching healthcare and research centers. More than ten thousands of Indian medical experts are working in hospitals outside their homeland. More than 41,000 Indian surgeons are practicing in the US alone. More than one out of five surgeons in the US is of Indian descent, serving the stereotype about the Asian citizens.

India follows a two-tier health distribution system. Because of the wide-spread poverty in the country, the public healthcare system provides medical care to the underprivileged section at little or zero cost.

Majority of Indian population does not have medical insurance. This causes hospitals to construct affordable healthcare packages. And there are only a few people in the world that can afford elective treatments or the expenses of the big surgeries. The low-cost treatment helps in attracting patients for medical treatment in India

The inflow of international patients in India has also motivated the hospitals to stay well-equipped with the latest technologies, as they have become a major source of profit for them. It also caused a positive impact on India tourism-boosting the economy.

The Growth of Medical Tourism in India

Medical Treatment industry in India is clipping along more than 25 percent growth rate for the countrys economy annually. India attracts the majority of patients from its cross-border travelers and its immediate neighbors including Bangladesh, the Middle East, the Turkic States, and East Africa. However, these numbers have also increased because of the rise of Canadians, Americans, and Europeans travelling to India for their treatment, particularly for more expensive producers like cardiac, neurology, and orthopedic surgeries. International patients are able to save thousands of dollars by receiving treatment in India instead of their own country.

Indias official national healthcare policies encourage foreign medical travel as a fragment of its economys export activities, although these services are delivered within India. The government utilizes the revenues produced from medical travel for increasing nations holdings in the foreign currency. The support and investment of the government and corporate sector solidifying behind the healthcare system, more multi-specialty and international hospitals are opening in the country every year.

Cardiac care is a substantial specialty delivered in India, with centers like Apollo (Chennai & New Delhi) and Fortis Wockhardt (Mumbai) leading the way. The success and morbidity rates delivered in India are at par with the statics achieved in the United States of America and Europe, with major procedures at only 15 percent of the cost.

Alternate Medical Treatment in India

India is the originator of several alternative medical treatments including Yoga and Ayurveda. Ayurveda has been known for providing a cure for several chronic ailments like Arthritis, Cancer, and Psoriasis etc. It can also effectively treat Nervous disorders.

Ayurveda treatment in India has existed since 5000 years and is considered one of the first medical treatment in the world. India comprises of some of best Ayurveda hospitals that have received accreditation from global organizations like NABH.

Why Receive Healthcare Treatment in India?

India has become the dominant medical travel destination in the world with over 250,000 patients visiting the country every year for obtaining treatment facilities for various medical treatments. The number of foreign patients getting medical treatment in India is expected to see an increase of 300,000 by 2020.

NABH & JCI Accredited (Quality Treatment)

Patients who are planning to receive treatment in India are typically interested in evaluating the quality of services which will be delivered to them at the hospital. International Society for Quality in Health Care aka ISQua creates a standard and framework for the medical facilities provided globally. JCI (Joint Commission International) and NABH (National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers) are two globally renowned and trusted healthcare quality councils accredited by ISQua.

Today India accounts for more than 34 JCI-accredited & over 425 NABH accredited hospitals.

Air Connectivity & E-VISA For Convenient Medical Travel to India

The metro-cities in India are connected by air transportation allowing patients to receive treatment without any delay, with global airlines operating in these cities, thus making medical treatment in India accessible to all. The Indian government has acknowledged the potential of medical tourism in India and has relaxed the visa norms, implementing E-visa policies. E-visa has a validity of 30 days that can be availed by tourists, for sightseeing and short trips for receiving medical treatment in India. Procedures which require longer duration need patients to obtain a medical visa that has a validity of 60 180 days whose cost starts at USD 85, which varies for each country. 

Availability of Cultural Facilities

India being amongst one of the worlds oldest civilization, has followers of most of the religions known to mankind. India is amongst the most religiously and culturally diverse country. This makes India a hospitable country with helpful, friendly people.

No Language Barrier

English is spoken widely across India, which makes it easier for foreign medical tourists to communicate their needs and medical condition in the country. Most hospitals also provide translators for several European and Arabic languages, whenever required.

Cultural Diversity

Indian cuisine is loved by people across the globe, and even if you dont like spicy food, India also has availability of different cuisines at its hotels and restaurants.

The cultural and biodiversity in India makes for some fascinating tourist spots in the country. Apart from being a major medical tourism destination, it is also a popular tourist spot.
